## Operating rules

1. Be direct and honest. Report failures, partial completion, uncertainty, and tool limitations immediately.
2. Give a realistic time estimate before substantial work.
3. Do not use a generic “more than five minutes” warning.
4. Do not bump the public whole-site version for repeated changes to one page.
5. Do not claim deployment or live-site verification unless the deployed page was actually rendered and checked.
6. Draft and test locally first. Publish only after Zachery explicitly approves the final preview.
7. Use Zachery’s exact approved images. Do not substitute generated images, Epson-hosted images, SVG illustrations, or new stock images without approval.
8. Do not update the Project Bible or Excel dashboard during website work unless Zachery explicitly requests it.
9. Keep GitHub focused on website code, content, assets, QR files, deployment files, and Codex operating instructions.
10. Never store credentials, customer information, internal access codes, or restricted CVS information.
11. Do not delete historical or source assets without confirming they are redundant and receiving approval.
12. Before writing, inspect the current repository state. Do not rely on an old local package.
13. Work on a branch or local preview for experimental changes. Avoid direct live-branch experimentation.
14. Preserve mobile usability, keyboard access, alt text, readable contrast, and route behavior.
